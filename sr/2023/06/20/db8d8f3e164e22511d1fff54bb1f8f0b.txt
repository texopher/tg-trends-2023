iFootball club
i
iAl Wahda Football Club (Arabic: نادي الوحدة لكرة القدم) is an Emirati
iprofessional football club based in Abu Dhabi, that competes in the UAE Pro
iLeague.[2] The club was founded in 1974 and plays its home games at the Al
iNahyan Stadium. The club's colours are maroon, navy blue and white.
i
i===== History =====
i
iThe first team created in Abu Dhabi was Al-Ahli in 1966, followed
irespectively by Al-Ittihad in 1968, Al-Falah and Al-Wahda in 1969. In 1974, a
idecision was made by the Minister of youth and sport to create Abu Dhabi SC
iby
imerging Al-Ittihad and Al-Wahda on 13 March 1974, and to create Al-Emirates
iSC
iby merging Al-Ahli and Al-Falah on 3 June 1974. In 1984, Abu Dhabi SC and
iAl-Emirates SC merged to create Al-Wahda FC.
i
iIn 1999, Al Wahda won their maiden UAE League title with 8 points ahead of
itheir rivals Al Ain. In 2018, Al Wahda changed its official logo as part of a
inew club redesign.

i===== Performance in AFC competitions =====
i
iAl Wahda has been qualifying for Asian competitions regularly since the
i1998–99 Asian Cup Winners' Cup, they've been qualifying for the AFC Champions
iLeague regularly by winning the President's Cup or consistently finishing
inear
ithe top in the league.
i
i===== Home stadium =====
i
iThe Al-Nahyan Stadium is the home of Al Wahda.[4] It has 15,000 seats and
ilocated only three kilometers from the downtown at the heart of Abu Dhabi
iCity.
