iLeandro Daniel Paredes (Spanish pronunciation: [leˈandɾo paˈɾeðes]; born 29
iJune 1994) is an Argentine professional footballer who plays as a defensive
imidfielder for Serie A club Juventus, on loan from Ligue 1 club Paris
iSaint-Germain, and the Argentina national team. He was part of the squad that
iwon the 2022 FIFA World Cup.
i
i===== Club career =====
i
iParedes made his league debut for Boca Juniors in a 2–0 away defeat to
iArgentinos Juniors on 6 November 2010. On 29 January 2014, he joined Italian
iSerie A outfit Chievo Verona for the rest of the 2013–14 season.[3]
i
iIt was revealed that Roma agreed to sign Paredes from Boca Juniors on loan
iwith a reported €4.5 million pre-set price, however Roma ran out of non-EU
iregistration quota for international transfer. He was signed by Chievo
iinstead.[4]
i
iOn 19 July 2014, Paredes joined Roma in a temporary deal, with an option to
ibuy outright from Boca Juniors.[5] On 27 September 2014, Paredes made his
iRoma
idebut, as a second-half substitute in the 2–0 win against Hellas Verona.[6]
iOn
i8 February 2015, Paredes scored his first Roma goal in the club's 2–1 victory
iagainst Cagliari.[7] In June 2015, Roma signed Paredes for €6.067 million.[8]
i
iOn 1 July 2017, Zenit announced the signing of Paredes on a four-year
icontract. The fee was €23 million with a possible €4 million in
iadd-ons.[9][10]
i
iOn 29 January 2019, Paris Saint-Germain announced the signing of Paredes on a
ifour and-a-half year contract,[11] for an initial €40 million, potentially
irising to €47 million.[12][13] He scored his first goal for the club in a 2–0
ivictory against Pau in the Coupe de France on 29 January 2020, exactly a year
iafter signing for PSG, while wearing the captain's armband.[14]
i
iOn 10 April 2021, Paredes scored his first Ligue 1 goal, a free-kick in a 4–1
iwin over Strasbourg.[15]
i
iOn 31 August 2022, Paredes joined Serie A club Juventus on a season-long loan
iwith an option to buy.[16]
i
i===== International career =====
i
iOn 19 May 2017, Paredes received his first senior call-up by newly appointed
icoach Jorge Sampaoli for Argentina's friendlies against Brazil and Singapore
iin June.[17] He made his senior international debut in the match against
iSingapore on 13 June, helping Argentina to a 6–0 away win, and also marked
ihis
idebut by scoring his first international goal.[18]
i
iIn May 2018, he was named in Argentina’s preliminary 35-man squad for the
i2018 FIFA World Cup in Russia[19] but did not make the final 23.[20] On 21
iMay
i2019, Paredes was included in Lionel Scaloni's final 23-man Argentina squad
ifor the 2019 Copa América.[21] Following Argentina's third-place achievement,
iParedes was listed among the "Best XI" team of the tournament.[22]
i
iIn June 2021, he was included in Lionel Scaloni's final Argentina 28-man
isquad for the 2021 Copa América.[23] In the semi-finals of the competition,
iArgentina played against Colombia and the match eventually went into a
ipenalty-shootout during which Paredes successfully converted his spot-kick to
ihelp Argentina progress to the final.[24]
i
iOn 11 November 2022, Paredes was named to Argentina's final 26-man squad for
ithe 2022 FIFA World Cup.[25] During Argentina's quarterfinal match against
ithe
iNetherlands, Paredes fouled Nathan Aké and deliberately smashed the ball into
ithe Dutch bench,[26] prompting a brawl. Paredes scored in the
ipenalty-shootout
iagainst the Netherlands winning 4–3 for Argentina after the game was tied 2–2
i(a.e.t). Paredes scored again in the penalty-shootout against France in the
iWorld Cup final. Winning the penalty-shootout 4–2, after the game was tied
i3–3
i(a.e.t).
i
i===== Personal life =====
i
iParedes is of Paraguayan descent through his mother and speaks fluent
iGuaraní.[27] Paredes' cousin, Franco, is also a professional footballer.[28]
