iMANILA, Philippines — San Juan City Vice Mayor Jose Warren Villa died on
iSunday evening, his niece Kirby Villa said early Monday morning.
i
iKirby did not disclose the cause of the vice mayor’s death but said it was
i“sudden and shocking” to them.
i
i“It is with great sadness that we announce the passing of our dearly beloved
iVice Mayor ‘VMWV’ Warren Villa, or as most of us call him, Daddy Warren. He
iwas the best daddy, kuya, and husband anyone could ask for,” she wrote in a
iFacebook post.
i
i“He changed our lives and, amazingly, the life of everyone he has met, both
iin his political career and private life. As his death was sudden and
ishocking
ito us, we humbly request that you allow us to process this as a family,” she
iadded.

iKirby also said they will release details of Villa’s wake as soon as arranged
iand finalized.
i
iEarlier, San Juan City Mayor Francis Zamora announced Villa’s death, saying
ithat the latter died with his wife Inah and Hab by his side.
i
i“It is with great sadness that I inform you that a highly respected and
imuch-loved member of the San Juan City Government, Vice Mayor Warren Villa,
ihas passed away this evening, with his wife Inah and son Hab by his side,”
ithe
imayor wrote in a Facebook post on Sunday.
i
i“Warren was a dear friend to me, and we are mourning the loss of a beloved
iSan Juaneño whose invaluable contribution to our city’s progress we will
iforever honor,” he added.
i
iZamora asked the public to pray for the bereaved family of Warren.
i
i“Please keep Warren’s family in your thoughts and prayers during this
iincredibly difficult time. Maraming salamat sa iyong kabutihan at
ipaglilingkod
isa ating mga mamamayan. Mahal ka namin, Warren,” he said.
i
i(Thank you very much for your kindness and service to our citizens. We love
iyou, Warren.)
i
iFollowing Villa’s death, Zamora announced in a separate Facebook post that
ithe San Juan City government events scheduled for June 19, such as the Wreath
iLaying Ceremony, Blood Letting Program, 50th Wedding Anniversary Renewal of
iVows, The Medical City Mobiclinic Launch, and the Feeding Program for
iBarangay
iCorazon De Jesus, are cancelled.
